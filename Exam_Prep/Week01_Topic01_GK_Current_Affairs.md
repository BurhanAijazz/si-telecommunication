## 1. Major National and International Events

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

This section tests your awareness of **recent happenings** in India and the world. Think of it as knowing what has been in the news over the last 12 to 18 months.

### Key Areas to Track

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Summits & Conferences** | G20, G7, BRICS, COP (Climate), SCO, ASEAN |
| **Awards & Honours** | Nobel Prizes, Bharat Ratna, Padma Awards, Dadasaheb Phalke |
| **Sports Events** | Olympics, Cricket World Cup, Asian Games, Commonwealth Games |

### Technical Deep-Dive: How to Study Current Affairs Effectively

1. **Daily habit:** Read one national newspaper or a current affairs app for 30 minutes.
2. **Monthly revision:** Use a monthly current affairs PDF or magazine.
3. **Categorize:** Sort news into Economy, Polity, Science, Sports, International.
4. **Focus on India-centric events** -- the exam is India-focused.

### Important Recent Highlights (Illustrative)

- **G20 Summit:** India held the G20 Presidency in 2023. The theme was **"One Earth, One Family, One Future"** (Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam). The New Delhi Leaders' Declaration was adopted by consensus. The **African Union** was admitted as a permanent member of G20.
- **BRICS Expansion:** At the 2023 Johannesburg Summit, BRICS invited six new members (effective January 2024): **Saudi Arabia, Iran, Ethiopia, Egypt, Argentina, and the UAE**. Argentina later declined.
- **Nobel Prizes (2024-2025):** Keep a table of recent laureates by category -- Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, Economics.
- **Paris Olympics 2024:** Key Indian medal winners and their sports.

> **Exam Tip:** Questions often ask "Which country hosted X summit?" or "Who won the Nobel Prize for Y in year Z?" Maintain a quick-reference table.

## 2. Indian Economy: Budget, Flagship Schemes, Reforms

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

The Indian economy section tests whether you understand how the government manages money, helps citizens, and grows the economy. You do not need to be an economist -- just know the **key terms, schemes, and numbers**.

### Key Economic Terms

| Term | Simple Meaning |
|------|---------------|
| **GDP (Gross Domestic Product)** | Total value of all goods and services produced in India in one year |
| **Fiscal Deficit** | When the government spends more than it earns |
| **Inflation** | Rise in prices of goods and services over time |
| **Repo Rate** | Interest rate at which RBI lends money to commercial banks |
| **FDI (Foreign Direct Investment)** | Money invested by foreign companies in India |
| **Disinvestment** | Government selling its shares in public sector companies |
| **GST (Goods and Services Tax)** | One unified indirect tax replacing multiple state and central taxes (introduced July 2017) |

### Union Budget Basics

The **Union Budget** is presented every year on **1st February** by the **Finance Minister**.

**Key components of the Budget:**

| Component | What It Covers |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Revenue Receipts** | Tax revenue + Non-tax revenue |
| **Capital Receipts** | Loans, disinvestment proceeds |
| **Revenue Expenditure** | Salaries, subsidies, interest payments |
| **Capital Expenditure** | Building roads, bridges, infrastructure |

### Major Flagship Schemes

| Scheme | Year Launched | Purpose |
|--------|:------------:|---------|
| **Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)** | 2014 | Financial inclusion -- bank accounts for all |
| **Swachh Bharat Mission** | 2014 | Clean India -- toilet construction, waste management |
| **Make in India** | 2014 | Boost manufacturing sector in India |
| **Skill India (PMKVY)** | 2015 | Train youth in employable skills |
| **PM Awas Yojana** | 2015 | Affordable housing for all |
| **Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY)** | 2018 | Health insurance of Rs 5 lakh per family per year |
| **PM Kisan Samman Nidhi** | 2019 | Rs 6,000/year to small and marginal farmers |
| **Atmanirbhar Bharat Abhiyan** | 2020 | Self-reliant India -- economic recovery package |
| **National Education Policy (NEP)** | 2020 | Reform school and higher education |
| **PM Gati Shakti** | 2021 | Integrated infrastructure master plan |
| **Agnipath Scheme** | 2022 | Short-term military recruitment (4 years) |
| **PM Vishwakarma** | 2023 | Support for traditional artisans and craftspeople |

### Economic Reforms to Know

- **GST Implementation (2017):** "One Nation, One Tax" -- replaced 17 indirect taxes.
- **Demonetisation (2016):** Rs 500 and Rs 1000 notes were withdrawn.
- **Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016:** Faster resolution of bad loans.
- **PLI Scheme (Production Linked Incentive):** Incentives to boost manufacturing in sectors like electronics, pharma, automobiles.

> **Exam Tip:** Remember the **launch year** and **objective** of each scheme. Questions typically follow the pattern: "Which scheme provides health coverage of Rs 5 lakh?"

## 3. Developments in Science & Technology

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

This section covers **breakthroughs and innovations** in science. You need a basic awareness of what India and the world have achieved recently.

### Key Areas

| Area | What to Study |
|------|--------------|
| **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** | ChatGPT, AI regulation, India's AI Mission |
| **Biotechnology** | mRNA vaccines, gene editing (CRISPR), genomic studies |
| **Quantum Computing** | National Quantum Mission (Rs 6,003 crore, approved 2023) |
| **Supercomputers** | India's PARAM series, top global supercomputers |
| **5G Technology** | Launched in India in October 2022 |
| **Semiconductor Mission** | India Semiconductor Mission to build chip fabrication plants |
| **Renewable Energy** | Solar, wind energy targets; Green Hydrogen Mission |

### Technical Deep-Dive: Key Concepts

**Artificial Intelligence (AI):**
- AI refers to machines that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence -- learning, reasoning, problem-solving.
- India launched the **IndiaAI Mission** with a budget of Rs 10,372 crore to build AI compute capacity and develop AI applications.

**CRISPR Gene Editing:**
- CRISPR-Cas9 is a tool that allows scientists to **edit DNA** with precision.
- Think of it like a molecular "find and replace" tool for genes.
- Applications: treating genetic diseases, improving crops, fighting infections.

**5G Technology:**
- **5G** = Fifth Generation mobile network.
- Offers speeds up to **10 Gbps** (100 times faster than 4G).
- Enables IoT (Internet of Things), smart cities, autonomous vehicles.

**Green Hydrogen:**
- Hydrogen produced using **renewable energy** (solar/wind) to split water into hydrogen and oxygen.
- India launched the **National Green Hydrogen Mission** (2023) with an outlay of Rs 19,744 crore.

> **Exam Tip:** Focus on India-specific launches and missions. Know the difference between 4G and 5G. Know what CRISPR, AI, and quantum computing mean in simple terms.

## 4. Space, Defence and Nuclear Programmes

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

India has a proud history of space, defence, and nuclear achievements. This is a **high-scoring** topic because the facts are fixed and easy to memorize.

### Indian Space Programme (ISRO)

**ISRO** = Indian Space Research Organisation
- **Founded:** 1969
- **Headquarters:** Bengaluru
- **Current Chairman:** Keep updated (as of early 2025, S. Somanath)

| Mission | Year | Significance |
|---------|:----:|-------------|
| **Aryabhata** | 1975 | India's first satellite |
| **SLV-3** | 1980 | India's first indigenous launch vehicle |
| **Chandrayaan-1** | 2008 | Discovered water molecules on the Moon |
| **Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM)** | 2014 | India became the first country to reach Mars orbit on its maiden attempt |
| **Chandrayaan-3** | 2023 | **Successful soft landing near the Moon's south pole** -- India became the 4th country to land on the Moon (after USA, Russia, China) |
| **Aditya-L1** | 2023 | India's first solar observatory mission; placed at Lagrange Point L1 |
| **Gaganyaan** | Upcoming | India's first crewed spaceflight mission |
| **NISAR** | 2025 | NASA-ISRO joint Earth observation satellite |

### Defence Programmes

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Tejas (LCA)** | Light Combat Aircraft -- India's indigenous fighter jet |
| **INS Vikrant** | India's first indigenously built aircraft carrier (commissioned 2022) |
| **BrahMos** | Supersonic cruise missile -- joint venture with Russia; world's fastest cruise missile |
| **Agni-V** | Intercontinental ballistic missile; range over 5,000 km |
| **S-400 Triumf** | Air defence missile system purchased from Russia |
| **Rafale** | Multi-role fighter jets acquired from France |
| **HAL AMCA** | Advanced Medium Combat Aircraft (5th generation stealth fighter, under development) |

### Nuclear Programme

- **First Nuclear Test (Smiling Buddha):** 1974 at Pokhran, Rajasthan
- **Pokhran-II (Operation Shakti):** 1998 -- five nuclear tests under PM Atal Bihari Vajpayee
- India has a **No First Use (NFU)** nuclear policy
- **Nuclear Triad:** India can launch nuclear weapons from land (Agni missiles), sea (INS Arihant submarine), and air (fighter jets)
- **Nuclear Command Authority:** Headed by the **Prime Minister** (Chairman of the Political Council)
- India is **not** a signatory to the **NPT (Non-Proliferation Treaty)** but signed the **CTBT** debate remains open

### Important Defence Organizations

| Organization | Role |
|-------------|------|
| **DRDO** | Defence Research and Development Organisation -- develops defence technologies |
| **HAL** | Hindustan Aeronautics Limited -- manufactures aircraft |
| **BEL** | Bharat Electronics Limited -- defence electronics |
| **ISRO** | Space missions and satellite launches |
| **DAE** | Department of Atomic Energy -- nuclear programme |

> **Exam Tip:** Chandrayaan-3 and Aditya-L1 are very likely exam questions. Know the year, objective, and outcome of each major mission.

## 5. Indian Polity

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

Indian Polity is the study of **how India is governed**. The Constitution of India is the supreme law. This section focuses on three key areas: the Preamble, Fundamental Rights & Duties, and Directive Principles.

### 5.1 The Preamble

The **Preamble** is the introduction to the Constitution. It declares the ideals and objectives of the Constitution.

**Full Text of the Preamble:**

> "WE, THE PEOPLE OF INDIA, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a **SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC** and to secure to all its citizens:
> **JUSTICE**, social, economic and political;
> **LIBERTY** of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship;
> **EQUALITY** of status and of opportunity;
> and to promote among them all **FRATERNITY** assuring the dignity of the individual and the unity and integrity of the Nation..."

**Key Facts:**

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Based on | Objectives Resolution moved by **Jawaharlal Nehru** (13 December 1946) |
| **"Socialist"** and **"Secular"** added by | **42nd Amendment (1976)** |
| **"Integrity"** added by | **42nd Amendment (1976)** |
| Is the Preamble part of the Constitution? | **Yes** (as per **Kesavananda Bharati case, 1973**) |
| Can the Preamble be amended? | **Yes**, but the **basic structure** cannot be destroyed |

### 5.2 Fundamental Rights (Part III, Articles 12-35)

Fundamental Rights are **legally enforceable** rights guaranteed to every citizen. They can be enforced through the courts (Article 32 -- Supreme Court; Article 226 -- High Court).

| Right | Article(s) | Key Provisions |
|-------|:----------:|---------------|
| **Right to Equality** | 14-18 | Equality before law; prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth; abolition of untouchability; abolition of titles |
| **Right to Freedom** | 19-22 | Six freedoms (speech, assembly, association, movement, residence, profession); protection of life and personal liberty; protection against arrest and detention |
| **Right against Exploitation** | 23-24 | Prohibition of human trafficking and forced labour; prohibition of child labour (below 14 years) in factories, mines, hazardous jobs |
| **Right to Freedom of Religion** | 25-28 | Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion |
| **Cultural and Educational Rights** | 29-30 | Protection of interests of minorities; right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions |
| **Right to Constitutional Remedies** | 32 | Right to move the Supreme Court for enforcement of Fundamental Rights (**"Heart and Soul of the Constitution"** -- Dr. B.R. Ambedkar) |

**Five Writs under Article 32:**

| Writ | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Habeas Corpus** | "Produce the body" -- against unlawful detention |
| **Mandamus** | "We command" -- orders a public official to perform duty |
| **Prohibition** | Stops a lower court from exceeding its jurisdiction |
| **Certiorari** | Transfers a case from lower court to higher court |
| **Quo Warranto** | "By what authority" -- questions the legality of a person holding public office |

> **Note:** Right to Property was originally a Fundamental Right (Article 31). It was removed by the **44th Amendment (1978)** and made a legal right under **Article 300A**.

### 5.3 Fundamental Duties (Part IV-A, Article 51A)

Fundamental Duties were added by the **42nd Amendment Act, 1976** on the recommendation of the **Swaran Singh Committee**. Originally **10 duties**; the **86th Amendment (2002)** added the 11th duty.

| No. | Duty (Simplified) |
|:---:|-------------------|
| 1 | Abide by the Constitution and respect the National Flag and National Anthem |
| 2 | Cherish and follow the noble ideals of the freedom struggle |
| 3 | Uphold and protect the sovereignty, unity, and integrity of India |
| 4 | Defend the country and render national service when called upon |
| 5 | Promote harmony and brotherhood; renounce practices derogatory to women |
| 6 | Value and preserve the rich heritage of our composite culture |
| 7 | Protect and improve the natural environment (forests, lakes, rivers, wildlife) |
| 8 | Develop the scientific temper, humanism, and spirit of inquiry and reform |
| 9 | Safeguard public property and abjure violence |
| 10 | Strive towards excellence in all spheres of individual and collective activity |
| 11 | **(Added by 86th Amendment, 2002)** Provide opportunities for education to children aged 6-14 years |

> **Key Point:** Fundamental Duties are **not enforceable by courts** (non-justiciable), unlike Fundamental Rights. However, they serve as moral obligations.

### 5.4 Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) (Part IV, Articles 36-51)

DPSPs are **guidelines** to the government for making laws and policies. They are **not enforceable by courts** (non-justiciable), but they are **fundamental in the governance** of the country.

**Classification of DPSPs:**

| Type | Articles | Examples |
|------|:--------:|---------|
| **Socialistic Principles** | 38, 39, 39A, 41, 42, 43, 43A, 47 | Equal justice, equal pay for equal work, right to work, free legal aid, living wage |
| **Gandhian Principles** | 40, 43, 46, 47, 48 | Village panchayats, cottage industries, promote weaker sections, prohibition of intoxicating drinks, protect cows |
| **Liberal-Intellectual Principles** | 44, 45, 48, 48A, 49, 50, 51 | Uniform Civil Code, free education for children, protect monuments, separate judiciary from executive, promote international peace |

**Comparison: Fundamental Rights vs. DPSP**

| Feature | Fundamental Rights | DPSP |
|---------|-------------------|------|
| **Part of Constitution** | Part III | Part IV |
| **Enforceable by Courts?** | Yes (Justiciable) | No (Non-justiciable) |
| **Nature** | Negative (restrict the state) | Positive (direct the state) |
| **Inspired by** | US Bill of Rights | Irish Constitution |
| **Purpose** | Protect individual liberty | Promote social welfare |

## 6. Disaster Management & Climate Change

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

**Disaster Management** is about preparing for, responding to, and recovering from natural and man-made disasters. **Climate change** refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, primarily caused by human activities (burning fossil fuels).

### Disaster Management in India

**Key Institution:** **National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA)**
- **Established:** 2005 under the **Disaster Management Act, 2005**
- **Chairman:** **Prime Minister of India**
- **Executive body:** National Disaster Response Force (**NDRF**)

**Three phases of Disaster Management:**

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|-----------|
| **Pre-Disaster (Preparedness)** | Planning, early warning systems, mock drills, capacity building |
| **During Disaster (Response)** | Evacuation, search and rescue, relief operations |
| **Post-Disaster (Recovery)** | Rehabilitation, reconstruction, lessons learned |

**Types of Disasters:**

| Natural Disasters | Man-Made Disasters |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Earthquakes | Industrial accidents (Bhopal Gas Tragedy, 1984) |
| Floods and cyclones | Nuclear disasters (Chernobyl, Fukushima) |
| Tsunamis (Indian Ocean, 2004) | Chemical spills |
| Landslides | Terrorist attacks |
| Droughts | Fire accidents |
| Avalanches | Oil spills |

**India's Disaster Vulnerability:**
- India is the **7th most disaster-prone country** globally.
- **Seismic Zones:** India is divided into **4 seismic zones** (Zone II to Zone V). Zone V is most vulnerable (includes parts of J&K, Himachal Pradesh, Northeast India).

### Climate Change

**Key Concepts:**

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Global Warming** | Rise in Earth's average temperature due to greenhouse gases |
| **Greenhouse Gases (GHGs)** | CO2, methane, nitrous oxide, CFCs -- trap heat in the atmosphere |
| **Carbon Footprint** | Total GHG emissions caused by an individual, organization, or product |
| **Paris Agreement (2015)** | Global agreement to limit temperature rise to **1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels** |
| **COP (Conference of Parties)** | Annual UN climate summit (COP28 was held in Dubai, UAE in 2023; COP29 in Baku, Azerbaijan in 2024) |
| **Net Zero** | Balancing GHG emissions with removal, so net emissions are zero |

**India's Climate Commitments:**

| Commitment | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Net Zero Target** | India aims to achieve net-zero emissions by **2070** (announced at COP26, Glasgow, 2021) |
| **Renewable Energy** | Target of **500 GW** non-fossil fuel electricity capacity by 2030 |
| **Panchamrit Goals** | Five climate action targets announced by PM Modi at COP26 |
| **National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)** | 8 National Missions (Solar, Enhanced Energy Efficiency, Sustainable Habitat, Water, Sustaining the Himalayan Ecosystem, Green India, Sustainable Agriculture, Strategic Knowledge for Climate Change) |
| **International Solar Alliance (ISA)** | Co-founded by India and France in 2015; HQ in **Gurugram, Haryana** |

> **Exam Tip:** Know the difference between NDMA and NDRF. Memorize India's net-zero target year (2070) and Paris Agreement's goal (1.5 degrees Celsius). The NAPCC's 8 missions are frequently asked.
