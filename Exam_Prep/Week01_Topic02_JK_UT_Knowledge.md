## 1. History of Jammu & Kashmir

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

The history of Jammu & Kashmir stretches from ancient times to the modern era. For the exam, you need to know the key dynasties, rulers, treaties, and the political evolution from a princely state to a Union Territory.

### 1.1 Ancient History

| Period | Key Facts |
|--------|-----------|
| **Neolithic Period** | Evidence of early human settlement found at **Burzahom** (near Srinagar) and **Gufkral** (Anantnag) -- pit dwellings, stone tools, and pottery discovered |
| **Mauryan Period (3rd century BCE)** | Emperor **Ashoka** is credited with founding the city of **Srinagar** (originally called **Srinagari**). He also introduced **Buddhism** to Kashmir |
| **Kushan Period (1st-3rd century CE)** | King **Kanishka** held the **Fourth Buddhist Council** at **Kundalvana, Kashmir** (presided by **Vasumitra**) |

### 1.2 Medieval History

| Dynasty/Ruler | Period | Key Contributions |
|--------------|:------:|------------------|
| **Karkota Dynasty** | 7th-9th century | Founded by **Durlabhvardhana**. Greatest ruler: **Lalitaditya Muktapida** -- built the famous **Martand Sun Temple** (Anantnag); expanded the empire across northern India |
| **Utpala Dynasty** | 9th-10th century | Founded by **Avantivarman**. His engineer **Suyya** controlled floods and reclaimed land in the Kashmir Valley |
| **Lohara Dynasty** | 11th-12th century | **Kalhana**, the great historian, wrote **Rajatarangini** ("River of Kings") during this period (1148-1150 CE). It is the **first systematic historical chronicle of Kashmir** |
| **Shah Mir Dynasty** | 1339-1561 | **Shah Mir** became the first Muslim ruler of Kashmir (Sultan Shamsuddin Shah Mir). This marked the beginning of Muslim rule in Kashmir |
| **Sultan Zain-ul-Abidin (Budshah)** | 1420-1470 | Called the **"Akbar of Kashmir"**. Known for religious tolerance, promotion of arts, crafts, and learning. He abolished **Jizya** tax and promoted **shawl weaving** and **papier-mache** |
| **Chak Dynasty** | 1561-1586 | Last independent dynasty of Kashmir before Mughal conquest |

### 1.3 Mughal, Afghan, and Sikh Period

| Period | Duration | Key Events |
|--------|:--------:|-----------|
| **Mughal Rule** | 1586-1752 | **Akbar** conquered Kashmir in 1586. The Mughals built the famous **Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi** gardens in Srinagar |
| **Afghan Rule (Durrani)** | 1752-1819 | Period of oppressive rule. Kashmir suffered under Afghan governors |
| **Sikh Rule** | 1819-1846 | **Ranjit Singh** conquered Kashmir in 1819. Sikh governors (like Gulab Singh) administered the region |

### 1.4 Dogra Rule (1846-1947)

| Event | Year | Significance |
|-------|:----:|-------------|
| **Treaty of Lahore** | March 1846 | After the First Anglo-Sikh War, the Sikh Empire ceded territories to the British |
| **Treaty of Amritsar** | 16 March 1846 | The British sold the Kashmir Valley to **Raja Gulab Singh** (Dogra ruler of Jammu) for **Rs 75 lakh (Nanakshahi)**. This made him the Maharaja of Jammu & Kashmir |
| **Dogra Dynasty** | 1846-1947 | Gulab Singh and his successors ruled J&K as a princely state under British paramountcy |
| **Maharaja Hari Singh** | Last ruler | The last Dogra ruler. Signed the **Instrument of Accession** on **26 October 1947**, acceding J&K to India |

### 1.5 Post-Independence History

| Event | Date | Details |
|-------|:----:|--------|
| **Instrument of Accession** | 26 October 1947 | Maharaja Hari Singh signed the Instrument of Accession to India after Pakistani tribal raiders invaded Kashmir |
| **First Kashmir War** | 1947-1948 | India sent forces to defend Kashmir. A **ceasefire line** was established (later called the **Line of Control**) |
| **UN Ceasefire** | 1 January 1949 | UN-mediated ceasefire came into effect |
| **Article 370** | 1949 | Granted **special autonomous status** to Jammu & Kashmir within the Indian Constitution |
| **Article 35A** | 1954 | Empowered the J&K legislature to define "permanent residents" and their special rights |
| **Sheikh Abdullah** | -- | Known as **"Sher-e-Kashmir"** (Lion of Kashmir); first Prime Minister of J&K; founded the **National Conference** party |
| **Shimla Agreement** | 1972 | After the 1971 war, India and Pakistan agreed to resolve disputes bilaterally; the ceasefire line was renamed the **Line of Control (LoC)** |
| **Abrogation of Article 370** | **5 August 2019** | Article 370 was effectively abrogated by a Presidential Order. J&K was reorganised into two Union Territories |

> **Exam Tip:** The Treaty of Amritsar (1846), Instrument of Accession (1947), Article 370, and the 2019 reorganisation are the most frequently tested topics.

## 2. Geography of Jammu & Kashmir

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

After the reorganisation in 2019, the erstwhile state of Jammu & Kashmir was split into two Union Territories: **J&K UT** (with legislature) and **Ladakh UT** (without legislature). This section covers the geography of J&K UT.

### 2.1 Basic Geographic Profile

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Area** | Approximately 42,241 sq km (J&K UT, excluding Ladakh) |
| **Districts** | **20 districts** (10 in Kashmir Division, 10 in Jammu Division) |
| **Capital** | **Srinagar** (summer capital) and **Jammu** (winter capital) |
| **Border Countries** | Pakistan (west), China (north, via Ladakh), and Afghanistan (in the Gilgit-Baltistan region under Pakistan's control) |
| **Latitude** | Between 32.17 degrees N and 36.58 degrees N |
| **Longitude** | Between 73.26 degrees E and 80.30 degrees E |

### Districts at a Glance

| Kashmir Division (10) | Jammu Division (10) |
|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Srinagar | Jammu |
| Budgam | Samba |
| Anantnag | Kathua |
| Baramulla | Udhampur |
| Pulwama | Reasi |
| Kupwara | Rajouri |
| Ganderbal | Poonch |
| Bandipora | Doda |
| Kulgam | Ramban |
| Shopian | Kishtwar |

### 2.2 Rivers

| River | Origin | Key Details |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Jhelum** | **Verinag Spring** (Anantnag district) | The **lifeline of the Kashmir Valley**; flows through Srinagar; passes through **Dal Lake** via the city; eventually flows into Pakistan; total length approx. 725 km |
| **Chenab** | **Confluence of Chandra and Bhaga rivers** at Tandi (Himachal Pradesh) | Largest river of J&K by volume; flows through Doda, Ramban, and Reasi districts |
| **Tawi** | Kailash Mountain (Bhaderwah area) | Flows through **Jammu city**; called the **"lifeline of Jammu"** |
| **Ravi** | Near Rohtang Pass (Himachal Pradesh) | Flows along the southern border of J&K (Kathua district) |
| **Poonch (Suran)** | Pir Panjal Range | Tributary of the Jhelum |
| **Lidder** | Kolahoi Glacier | Flows through Pahalgam; a tributary of the Jhelum |
| **Sindh** | Machoi Glacier (near Sonamarg) | Joins the Jhelum at Shadipora near Srinagar |

> **Memory Aid:** Remember **"J-C-T-R"** for the four major rivers: **J**helum, **C**henab, **T**awi, **R**avi.

### 2.3 Lakes

| Lake | Location | Key Features |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Dal Lake** | Srinagar | Called the **"Jewel in the Crown of Kashmir"** or **"Srinagar's Jewel"**; famous for houseboats and shikaras; area approx. 18 sq km |
| **Wular Lake** | Bandipora | **Largest freshwater lake in India** (by area); fed by the Jhelum River; area approx. 189 sq km (varies seasonally); designated as a **Ramsar Wetland site** |
| **Manasbal Lake** | Ganderbal | Called the **"Supreme Gem of all Kashmir Lakes"**; known for lotus blooms |
| **Nagin Lake** | Srinagar | Adjacent to Dal Lake; known for crystal-clear water |
| **Pangong Tso** | Ladakh UT | (Now in Ladakh UT, but may be asked) -- a long, narrow lake at 4,350 m altitude; one-third in India, two-thirds in China |
| **Sheshnag Lake** | Anantnag | High-altitude glacial lake on the Amarnath Yatra route |
| **Mansar Lake** | Udhampur (Jammu) | A sacred lake; associated with the epic of Arjuna and the legend of Shesha Nag |
| **Surinsar Lake** | Jammu | Along with Mansar, forms the **Surinsar-Mansar Wetland** (Ramsar site since 2005) |

### 2.4 Climate

J&K has diverse climatic conditions due to its varied topography.

| Region | Climate Type | Temperature Range | Features |
|--------|-------------|:-----------------:|---------|
| **Kashmir Valley** | Temperate (moderate) | Summer: 15-30 degrees C, Winter: -5 to 10 degrees C | Four distinct seasons; heavy snowfall in winter; called "Paradise on Earth" |
| **Jammu Region (Plains)** | Sub-tropical | Summer: 25-46 degrees C, Winter: 5-20 degrees C | Hot summers, moderate winters; monsoon rainfall |
| **Jammu Region (Hills)** | Temperate to Alpine | Varies with altitude | Cooler temperatures at higher elevations |
| **Pir Panjal & Higher Altitudes** | Alpine/Sub-Arctic | Below 0 degrees C in winter | Heavy snowfall; glaciers present |

**Seasons in Kashmir:**

| Local Name | Season | Months |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Sonth** | Spring | March-April |
| **Grishm (Raetikaal)** | Summer | May-June |
| **Waharat** | Autumn | September-October |
| **Wand** | Winter | November-February |

### 2.5 Soils

| Soil Type | Where Found | Characteristics |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Alluvial Soil** | Kashmir Valley floor, river banks | Fertile; good for rice, saffron, and horticulture |
| **Karewa Soil** | Elevated plateaus in Kashmir | Unique to Kashmir; **Karewas are lacustrine deposits** (old lake sediments); excellent for **saffron cultivation** (Pampore area) and almond/walnut orchards |
| **Mountain/Forest Soil** | Hill slopes, forested areas | Rich in humus; supports dense forests |
| **Red and Brown Soil** | Parts of Jammu region | Moderately fertile |
| **Glacial Soil** | High-altitude areas | Thin, rocky, low fertility |

> **Exam Tip:** **Karewa soils** are a very popular exam topic. Remember: Karewa = old lake deposits = saffron cultivation = Pampore.

### 2.6 Flora and Fauna

**Flora (Plants and Trees):**

| Vegetation Type | Altitude/Zone | Examples |
|----------------|:-------------:|---------|
| **Tropical/Sub-tropical** | Below 1,000 m (Jammu plains) | Sal, bamboo, mango, banyan, neem |
| **Temperate Forests** | 1,000-3,500 m | **Chinar** (oriental plane tree, symbol of Kashmir), deodar, pine (Kail), walnut, maple, willow, poplar |
| **Alpine Meadows** | 3,500-4,500 m | Grasslands, wild flowers, medicinal herbs; called **"marg"** (e.g., Gulmarg, Sonamarg) |
| **Cash Crops** | Various | **Saffron** (Pampore), apples (Sopore -- "Apple Town of Kashmir"), almonds, walnuts, cherries |

**Fauna (Animals and Birds):**

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **State Animal (J&K UT)** | **Hangul** (Kashmir Stag) -- an endangered species of deer found in **Dachigam National Park** |
| **State Bird** | **Black-necked Crane** (also associated with Ladakh) |
| **Mammals** | Himalayan brown bear, snow leopard, musk deer, markhor, ibex |
| **Birds** | Himalayan monal, bulbul, kingfisher, migratory birds at Hokersar Wetland |

**Important National Parks and Wildlife Sanctuaries:**

| Name | Location | Known For |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Dachigam National Park** | Srinagar | **Hangul (Kashmir Stag)** -- one of the last habitats |
| **Hemis National Park** | Ladakh UT | Snow leopard (largest national park in India) |
| **Kishtwar National Park** | Kishtwar | Snow leopard, Himalayan brown bear |
| **Salim Ali National Park** | Srinagar (near Dal Lake) | Named after the "Birdman of India"; bird watching |
| **Gulmarg Wildlife Sanctuary** | Baramulla | Musk deer, Himalayan black bear |
| **Hokersar Wetland** | Srinagar | Migratory birds (Ramsar site) |
| **Overa-Aru Wildlife Sanctuary** | Anantnag | Hangul, brown bear |

## 3. Jammu & Kashmir Reorganisation Act, 2019

### What You Need to Know (Beginner Level)

This is one of the most **landmark legislations** in India's recent history. On **5 August 2019**, the Government of India took a historic step that fundamentally changed the constitutional status of the erstwhile state of Jammu & Kashmir.

### 3.1 Background

| Term | Explanation |
|------|------------|
| **Article 370** | A "temporary provision" in the Indian Constitution that granted J&K special autonomous status. It allowed J&K to have its own constitution, a separate flag, and autonomy over all matters except defence, foreign affairs, and communications |
| **Article 35A** | Inserted in 1954 via a Presidential Order (not through a Constitutional Amendment by Parliament). It empowered the J&K legislature to define "permanent residents" and gave them special rights (e.g., only permanent residents could own property, get government jobs, or receive scholarships) |

### 3.2 What Happened on 5 August 2019

**Step-by-step sequence:**

1. **Presidential Order (C.O. 272):** On 5 August 2019, the President of India issued a new Constitutional Order (The Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 2019) that superseded the 1954 order. This effectively made **all provisions of the Indian Constitution applicable to J&K**.

2. **Abrogation of Article 370:** A resolution was moved in the **Rajya Sabha** (and later the Lok Sabha) recommending that all clauses of Article 370 (except Clause 1) be declared inoperative. The resolution was passed by both Houses of Parliament.

3. **Article 35A:** Since it was derived from Article 370, it became automatically void once Article 370 was rendered inoperative.

4. **J&K Reorganisation Bill:** The **Jammu and Kashmir Reorganisation Bill, 2019** was introduced and passed by Parliament. It received **Presidential assent on 9 August 2019**.

### 3.3 Key Provisions of the J&K Reorganisation Act, 2019

| Provision | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Date of Effect** | **31 October 2019** (the appointed day) -- chosen to coincide with the birth anniversary of **Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel** (National Unity Day) |
| **Two New UTs Created** | (1) **Union Territory of Jammu & Kashmir** (with a Legislative Assembly) and (2) **Union Territory of Ladakh** (without a legislature) |
| **Lieutenant Governor** | Each UT is headed by a **Lieutenant Governor (LG)** appointed by the President |
| **J&K Legislative Assembly** | J&K UT has a legislature with **90 seats** (previously 87, increased by 7 -- 6 nominated seats removed but total increased to 90 elected seats per Delimitation Commission 2022). Out of these, 24 seats are reserved for areas under Pakistan-occupied Kashmir (PoK) |
| **Ladakh UT** | Governed directly by the **LG and the Central Government** with no elected legislature |
| **High Court** | The **J&K and Ladakh High Court** (common for both UTs) continues with its principal seat in **Srinagar** and a bench in **Jammu** |
| **Jurisdiction of Indian Laws** | All central laws now fully applicable to J&K -- including the Indian Penal Code (IPC), Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), Right to Information (RTI), Right to Education (RTE), etc. |
| **Domicile Rules** | New domicile rules replaced the old "permanent resident" concept. The domicile certificate is now available to a broader category of persons |
| **Property Rights** | Non-residents of J&K can now purchase property (the earlier restriction under Article 35A no longer applies) |

### 3.4 Impact of the Reorganisation

| Area | Before (Special Status) | After (Reorganisation) |
|------|------------------------|----------------------|
| **Constitutional Status** | State with special status under Article 370 | Union Territory with legislature |
| **Own Constitution** | J&K had its own Constitution | No separate constitution; governed under the Indian Constitution |
| **Separate Flag** | J&K had its own state flag | Only the Indian Tricolour is the official flag |
| **Laws** | Many central laws did not apply | All central laws apply |
| **Property Rights** | Only "permanent residents" could buy property | Open to all Indian citizens |
| **Reservation** | Own reservation policies | Central reservation policies apply (SC/ST/OBC) |
| **Fundamental Rights** | Some Fundamental Rights were restricted | All Fundamental Rights fully applicable |
| **Governor vs LG** | Headed by a Governor (as a state) | Headed by a **Lieutenant Governor** |

### 3.5 Delimitation Commission (2022)

After the reorganisation, a **Delimitation Commission** was set up to redraw constituency boundaries in J&K UT.

| Key Detail | Information |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Chairperson** | Justice (Retd.) Ranjana Prakash Desai |
| **Total Assembly Seats** | **90** (previously 87 in the erstwhile state; 24 remain reserved for PoK) |
| **Jammu Division** | Got **43 seats** (previously 37) |
| **Kashmir Division** | Got **47 seats** (previously 46) |
| **Reserved Seats** | **7 seats for SCs** (all in Jammu region); **9 seats for STs** (across both divisions) -- first time STs got reservation in J&K |

### 3.6 Supreme Court Verdict (December 2023)

On **11 December 2023**, a five-judge Constitution Bench of the Supreme Court upheld the abrogation of Article 370, ruling that:

- Article 370 was a **"temporary provision"** and its abrogation was constitutionally valid.
- The President had the power to declare all provisions of the Constitution applicable to J&K.
- The Court directed that **elections to the J&K Legislative Assembly must be held by 30 September 2024**.
- The Court recommended setting up a **Truth and Reconciliation Commission** for addressing the grievances of people affected by the decades of conflict.

> **Exam Tip:** The date **31 October 2019** (appointed day), the two UTs created, the Delimitation details (90 seats, 43 Jammu + 47 Kashmir), and the Supreme Court's 2023 verdict are very important.
